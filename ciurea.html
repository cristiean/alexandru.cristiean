Ciurea commune

Ciurea is situated South of Iași, being one of the county's largest communes.

Population: 10954 in 2002, 12604 in 2012. 87,6% of Ciurea's population is Romanian, with a noticeable community of 6,1% Romani ethnics.

Component villages

  • Ciurea Village– attested at the end of the 17th century (1654), documented as having a colonising population of Ukranian and Hungarian origins, as well as former servants (called sloboziți, Romanian word for freedmans) of monasteries and boyars. Some version hold that the name comes from a boyar named Ciurel.
  • Lunca Cetățuii– historically present from the 18th century, with time englobing the Baba Necula hamlet. It used to be a [stație de post– missing translation] linking Iași to Vaslui.
