Ciurea commune

Ciurea is situated South of Iași, being one of the county's largest communes.

Ciurea's population was 10954 in 2002, and 12604 in 2012 (National Institute of Statistics). 87,6% of Ciurea's population is Romanian, with an important community of 6,1% Romani ethnics, according to the Housing and Population census in 2011.

Component villages

  • Ciurea Village– historically attested at the end of the seventeenth century (1654), documented as having a colonising population of Ukranian and Hungarian origins, as well as former servants of monasteries and boyars (called sloboziți, the Romanian word for freedmans). Another version holds that the name comes from a boyar named Ciurel.
